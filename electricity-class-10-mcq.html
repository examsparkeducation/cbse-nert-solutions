Pro Tips for Solving Electricity MCQs

To maximize your speed and accuracy during the exam, keep these golden rules in mind:

  1. Watch the Units: Always convert time to seconds and current to Amperes before applying formulas like \( H = I^2Rt \).
  2. Ammeter vs. Voltmeter: Remember that an Ammeter is always connected in series, and a Voltmeter is always connected in parallel. Examiners love testing this concept!
  3. Resistivity is Constant: The resistivity of a material does not change with its length or thickness; it only changes with temperature. This is a classic trick question.
  4. Eliminate Options: If a circuit is in parallel, the total resistance must be less than the smallest individual resistor. Use this logic to quickly eliminate mathematically impossible MCQ options.
