Chapter-wise Breakdown of Highly Tested Topics

To help you structure your revision, our expert educators at QSpark AI have analyzed past 10 years of CBSE question papers. Here is the unit-wise breakdown of the most critical concepts you must practice:

1. Number Systems (Real Numbers)

This unit is highly scoring and straightforward. Focus heavily on:

  • The fundamental theorem of arithmetic.
  • Finding the LCM and HCF of numbers and understanding their relationship (LCM × HCF = Product of two numbers).
  • Proving the irrationality of numbers like √2, √3, and √5.

2. Algebra (Polynomials, Linear Equations, Quadratic Equations, AP)

Algebra carries the highest weightage in the Class 10 board exam. The class 10 maths important questions in this section will test your calculation speed and logical reasoning.

  • Polynomials: Finding the zeroes of a quadratic polynomial and verifying the relationship between zeroes and coefficients.
  • Linear Equations: Word problems related to age, fractions, and upstream/downstream speed. Graphical method of solving equations.
  • Quadratic Equations: Finding roots using factorization and the quadratic formula. Analyzing the nature of roots using the discriminant (D = b² - 4ac).
  • Arithmetic Progression (AP): Finding the nth term (an) and the sum of n terms (Sn) of an AP, especially application-based word problems.

3. Trigonometry

Trigonometry is introduced for the first time in Class 10, making it both fresh and slightly daunting. However, it is highly systematic.

  • Introduction to Trigonometry: Memorizing the trigonometric ratios of specific angles (0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°). Proving standard trigonometric identities (like sin²θ + cos²θ = 1).
  • Applications (Heights and Distances): Case-study questions involving angles of elevation and depression. Drawing accurate diagrams is crucial here.

4. Coordinate Geometry

This is a highly visual and formula-driven chapter. Ensure you practice questions involving:

  • The Distance Formula to find the distance between two points or prove geometric shapes (like showing points form a square).
  • The Section Formula to find coordinates of a point dividing a line segment in a given ratio.

5. Geometry (Triangles and Circles)

Geometry requires strong conceptual clarity and the ability to write structured proofs.

  • Triangles: The Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales Theorem) and its converse. Questions based on the criteria for similarity of triangles.
  • Circles: Proving that the lengths of tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal. Questions involving angles between tangents and radii.

6. Mensuration (Surface Areas and Volumes)

This chapter tests your ability to memorize formulas and calculate accurately without making silly arithmetic errors. Important questions usually involve combinations of solids—for example, a cone surmounted on a hemisphere or a cylinder with hemispherical ends.

7. Statistics and Probability

This unit is often the saving grace for students as it provides straightforward, high-scoring questions.

  • Statistics: Calculating the Mean (Direct and Assumed Mean Method), Median, and Mode of grouped data. Understanding the empirical relationship between the three measures.
  • Probability: Theoretical probability of events like tossing coins, rolling dice, or drawing cards from a standard deck.

Common Mistakes to Avoid in the Maths Board Exam

Even brilliant students lose marks due to careless errors. Keep these tips in mind while practicing your class 10 maths important questions:

  • Not writing the formulas: Always state the formula you are using before substituting values. It carries step-marks.
  • Ignoring units: In Mensuration and Word problems, forgetting to write "cm²" or "km/hr" at the end of the final answer will cost you half a mark.
  • Poor diagram skills: In Geometry and Heights & Distances, always draw a neat diagram using a pencil and ruler. A correct diagram clarifies your thought process to the examiner.
  • Not checking the final calculation: Save 15 minutes at the end of the exam to double-check your addition, subtraction, and multiplication.
