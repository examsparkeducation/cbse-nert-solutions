Chemistry: The Science of Reactions (Chapters 1-4)

Chemistry in Class 10 is foundational and highly scoring. You must practice balancing equations and understanding chemical properties. Here are the core areas to focus on:

1. Chemical Reactions and Equations

Focus on balancing complex chemical equations. Important questions usually revolve around identifying the type of reaction (Combination, Decomposition, Displacement, Double Displacement) from a given chemical equation. Do not skip the concepts of Oxidation-Reduction (Redox) reactions, Corrosion, and Rancidity.

2. Acids, Bases, and Salts

This chapter is famous for practical-based questions. Practice questions on the reaction of acids/bases with metals, metal carbonates, and hydrogen carbonates. You must thoroughly memorize the preparation, chemical formulas, and uses of Bleaching powder, Baking soda, Washing soda, and Plaster of Paris (POP).

3. Metals and Non-Metals

The most important questions here come from the Reactivity Series and the extraction of metals (Metallurgy). Be prepared to answer reasoning questions on why certain metals displace others, the formation of ionic bonds (like NaCl and MgCl2), and the processes of Roasting and Calcination.

4. Carbon and its Compounds

This is arguably the most crucial chemistry chapter. Focus your practice on IUPAC nomenclature, homologous series, and the versatile nature of carbon. High-weightage 5-mark questions frequently feature the chemical properties of carbon compounds, specifically Saponification, Esterification, and the differences between soaps and detergents.

Biology: The Study of Life (Chapters 5-8)

Biology requires a mix of factual memorization and the ability to draw neat, well-labeled diagrams. Diagrams carry dedicated step-marks, so practice them diligently.

5. Life Processes

This is a lengthy chapter with massive weightage. Generate and practice questions on the human digestive system, the mechanism of breathing, double circulation in the human heart, and the structure/function of a nephron. Differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration, and arteries vs. veins are classic exam favorites.

6. Control and Coordination

Focus on the human brain (functions of cerebrum, cerebellum, and medulla), reflex arcs, and the functions of various plant hormones (Auxins, Gibberellins, Cytokinins, Abscisic acid). Questions on animal hormones, specifically insulin, thyroxine, and adrenaline, are highly recurring.

7. How do Organisms Reproduce?

Important questions in this chapter test your knowledge of both asexual and sexual reproduction. Key topics include vegetative propagation, the structure of a flower (pollination and fertilization), and the male and female human reproductive systems. Do not ignore the topics of contraception and reproductive health.

8. Heredity

Though shortened in the rationalized syllabus, Heredity is deeply conceptual. Practice questions on Mendel’s experiments (Monohybrid and Dihybrid crosses) and sex determination in human beings. You must be able to write down the genotypic and phenotypic ratios accurately.

Physics: Understanding the Universe (Chapters 9-12)

Class 10 Physics is highly numerical-driven. Memorizing formulas and understanding sign conventions is the key to mastering this section.

9. Light - Reflection and Refraction

This chapter guarantees numericals. Practice drawing ray diagrams for concave/convex mirrors and lenses for all object positions. Important questions heavily feature the Mirror Formula, Lens Formula, magnification, and the concept of Refractive Index.

10. The Human Eye and the Colourful World

Expect reasoning-based questions here. Why does the sky appear blue? Why do stars twinkle? What is the cause of a rainbow? Also, practice numericals and ray diagrams related to vision defects like Myopia, Hypermetropia, and Presbyopia, and their corrections using respective lenses.

11. Electricity

A pure numerical chapter. Our important questions class 10 science chapter wise analysis shows that Ohm's Law, calculating equivalent resistance in series and parallel circuits, and Joule’s law of heating are the most tested concepts. Always remember to state the SI units in your final answers.

12. Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

Focus on the magnetic field lines around a straight conductor, a circular loop, and a solenoid. Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule and the Right-Hand Thumb Rule are absolute must-knows for solving objective and short-answer questions. Understand the working principle of a domestic electric circuit and the function of an earth wire.
